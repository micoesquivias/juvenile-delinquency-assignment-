Republic Act 9344, also known as the "Juvenile Justice and Welfare Act of 2006," is a law in the Philippines that addresses the issue of juvenile delinquency.

Key Features of RA 9344

Minimum Age of Criminal Responsibility: The law sets the minimum age of criminal responsibility at 15 years old. This means that children below 15 years old are exempt from criminal liability.

Diversion Program: Instead of facing criminal charges, children who are 15 years old and above but below 18 years old at the time of the commission of the offense may undergo a diversion program. This program aims to rehabilitate the child and prevent further offenses.

Youthful Offender System: For those aged 15 to below 18 who are found guilty of an offense and are sentenced to serve imprisonment, they are placed in a youth care facility separate from adult offenders.
